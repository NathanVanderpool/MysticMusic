Most songs that move us were made by people who didn't know what they were doing until the song was finished. Not because they were unskilled — but because they were listening for something they couldn't name yet, and they followed it anyway.

Do you feel something in you that wants to be a song, but doesn't know how to become one?

Have you been writing for years but find yourself circling the same territory without going deeper?

Is there a gap between what you hear inside and what ends up on the page?

Do you want your music to mean something — and not quite know how to make that happen?

You don't have to be a songwriter to join. You don't have to have written a single song. If you have a feeling that music is somewhere in you and you haven't found the door yet — this course is exactly for that.

Mystic Music is an in-person songwriting course for people ready to work from the inside out. Not from formulas. Not from genre conventions. From the actual texture of your inner life — and the craft to carry it into sound.

This course is for you if

  • You want to write songs but have never started — or don't know how
  • You've written before and want to go somewhere you haven't been
  • You feel drawn to folk, singer-songwriter, or intimate acoustic music
  • You want to make music that is honest, not just impressive
  • You have feelings that need form and don't know how to give them one
  • You feel blocked around your voice and want a safe place to open it
  • You want to understand how melody, lyrics, and harmony work together
  • You want to finish something — and share it with people who matter

The sessions

01

Arrival & First Songs

Making it safe to be a beginner

The first evening is about making it safe to be a beginner. We get to know each other and the space, share why we're here and what draws us to songwriting. Through simple singing and writing games — low stakes, often funny — we start to lower the threshold and build trust in the group.

We introduce the spirit of the whole course: unvarnished enthusiasm, full commitment, no sugar-coating. The muse doesn't wait for you to feel ready — and the desk is where you meet her.

Showing up Pleasure as compass The muse and the desk
Take a melody you love — reshape it. Make it yours.
02

Melody First

Finding the thing before you know what it means

We sing over a drone. Gibberish is welcome. La-la-la is not. The point is to find a melody that wants to exist — without the pressure of having something to say yet.

Melodies get passed between partners and small groups, played with, transformed, until they become something different — something yours. In the second half, words start to attach to the melody naturally, even if they don't make sense yet. We follow the shape of speech, the way emotion lives in a long held note.

This session makes space for anyone who feels blocked around their voice or scared of sounding bad.

Stealing as a starting place Natural inflection of speech Long notes = emotion Melody before meaning
Take a melody you love — rewrite the lyrics.
03

Lyrics First

The question of whether something is too personal for a song — it isn't

Someone shares an intense or meaningful story. The group witnesses it fully — not to fix or advise, but to be truly present. Then everyone writes: a couplet, a few lines, some detail or phrase that struck them from what they heard.

We do this a few times. Then: here is a poem — pick one line that moves you and write two or three lines continuing from there. In the second half, we speak our lyrics rhythmically and begin to find their melody.

This session holds space for vulnerability, for the fear of exposure, for the question of whether something is too personal to put in a song.

Transmutation Plain language Simplicity Writing in your mother tongue
Take lyrics you love — rewrite the melody.
04

Chords & Context

Two chords is enough. Context changes everything.

This session demystifies the harmonic side of songwriting — how to steal a chord progression without anyone noticing, how simple instrument basics can unlock a whole new relationship to your material.

We look at how the same melody changes completely depending on what's underneath it, and practice putting it all together: melody, lyrics, and harmony in the same room at the same time.

Two chords is enough Context changes everything Stealing progressions Simple instrument tips
Bring a song — or a fragment of one — to the showcase.
05

The Show

Not a class — a concert

The final evening is not a class. It's a concert.

We take over a bar in Berlin. Ronja and Nathan perform as a band — and if you want, they're your band too. Anyone who wants to share what they made during the course can perform their song with full live backing. You bring the song; they follow you.

The space is safe and celebratory. We only name what we love. Nerves are welcome and will be held — this course is about songwriting, not performance, and if you just want to listen, that's completely fine.

Guests are welcome. Bring someone who should hear what you made.

The teachers

Nathan Vanderpool

Songwriter · Musician · Ceremony holder

Nathan has been writing and playing for over thirty years. He works at the intersection of song, contemplative practice, and community — his project Agapic Chants creates community healing songs through intimate 1:1 songwriting sessions. He holds a PhD in sociology and is deeply involved in the Church of Interbeing, a secular-sacred community in Berlin. His music is honest, unhurried, and made to mean something.

nathanvanderpool.com →

Ronja Polzin

Visual artist · Songwriter · Co-creator, Pappelbaum

Ronja Polzin is a Berlin-based visual artist and songwriter. Her work moves between image, text, and performance — often in the spaces where disciplines dissolve into each other. She and Nathan co-created Pappelbaum, an audio-visual project whose debut EP each eternal day was released in late 2025. She brings a visual artist's eye to the question of how inner experience finds form.
